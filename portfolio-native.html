Native iOS and Android for Milwaukee Tool

Industries: eCommerce, Manufacturing

The Challenge

Milwaukee Tool is a power tool company based in Brookfield, Wisconsin. They had a number of ongoing projects and new developments they wanted to bring to ONE-KEY, their bluetooth enabled power tool platform, but did not have the resources to develop all needed screens.

The Process

I worked with Milwaukee Tool for sixteen months, September 2015 to December 2017. Using a style guide I had previously developed, I split the iOS and Android apps from one style to two distinct applications. Over the course of a year, we were able to nearly completely redesign the apps.

While also updating older screens, the majority of my time was spent on adding or changing workflows. I personally developed a new on-boarding and login process, humanized their legalese, updated their “Tool Connect” and “Add Item” workflows and several smaller other projects. I worked alongside several developers and industrial designers on a number of physical products.

Methods

  • Visual Design (incorporating Human Interface Guidelines and Material Design)
  • Journey Mapping / Workflows
  • Mockups
  • Prototyping
  • Style Guide

Results

Customer reviews on the Android app store went up from 2.7 to 3.6 (out of five stars). The iOS application went from a 3.9 to a 4.1. We were able to completely modernize their style and quickly iterated through new projects, instead of redesigning everything.
